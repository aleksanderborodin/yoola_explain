How it works

A browser extension that waits for you to ask — it never summarizes on its own.

1It notices, quietly

A small tab appears on terms pages — and on signup forms that link to them, where Yoola reviews the linked documents without you leaving the page.

2One reading serves everyone

The server fetches the public document and analyzes it once. Everyone after gets the cached verdict instantly — and it lands in this directory.

3Every claim is quoted

Fourteen clause checks — arbitration, auto-renewal, data sale, and more. A claim counts only when the exact source sentence backs it; anything uncertain is marked “possible”, honestly.

D

Our own terms score a D.

We ran Yoola on Yoola's own terms: no warranties, liability capped at ten dollars, no class actions. Most software terms look like this — the difference is that we're the ones telling you, with the clauses quoted, before you agree.

“…PROVIDED STRICTLY ON AN ‘AS IS’ AND ‘AS AVAILABLE’ BASIS, WITH ALL FAULTS AND WITHOUT WARRANTY OF ANY KIND WHATSOEVER.” — Yoola ToS §4.1, flagged by Yoola: high severity, verified.
