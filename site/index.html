How it reads the fine print

Yoola never summarizes on its own — it waits for you to ask. Then:

1

It notices, quietly

A small tab appears on terms pages — and on signup pages that link to them, where Yoola can review the linked documents without you leaving the form. Detection runs entirely in your browser.

2

One reading serves everyone

Our server fetches the public document itself and analyzes it once. Every later visitor gets the same summary instantly, from cache. Nobody's page contents are uploaded.

3

Every claim is quoted

Fourteen clause checks — arbitration, auto-renewal, data sale, and more. A claim only counts as verified when the exact source sentence anchors it. Anything uncertain is marked “possible”, honestly.

The fourteen checks

Every document is examined against the same checklist, so grades are comparable across sites. The four in red are high-stakes.

Arbitration & class-action waiver Unilateral changes to terms Automatic renewal & billing Sale or sharing of personal data License over your content Limitation of liability Account termination Governing law & venue Refunds Indemnification Tracking & profiling Children & age limits Warranty disclaimer Data & account deletion

If a document is silent on a check, Yoola says “not addressed” — silence is information too.

Our own terms score a D.

D

We ran Yoola on Yoola's own terms of service — the long, lawyer-grade document that governs this tool. Verdict: D, harsh terms. No warranties, liability capped at ten dollars, no class actions. Like most software terms, ours protect the operator. The difference is that we're the ones telling you, in plain language, with the clauses quoted — before you agree. That's the product.
